# Hurricane Turbine — Passive Roof Ventilator

## Overview
The Hurricane is a passive (wind-driven) roof ventilator used to exhaust hot air out of industrial, commercial, and institutional buildings. It has no motor and no electrical components — it uses wind over the roof to rotate and draw warm stale air up and out of the building. It comes in four throat sizes (H300, H600, H700, H900) with a choice of straight flue, vari-pitch flue, and optional roof-operated damper. Wind speed rating is 205.2 km/h (57 m/s) per AS 4740:2000 Performance Level 1.

## Best Applications
- Large warehouses venting hot ceiling air (case study: 25 Hurricanes used to vent an industrial storage facility on the Great Lakes)
- Manufacturing plants venting production heat
- Institutional and commercial buildings that want passive ventilation with no power consumption
- Facilities that want ventilation in shoulder seasons without running fans

## Key Features & Benefits
- **Passive** — no power required, no electrical installation
- Free-spinning under wind load
- Rounded dome prevents weather ingress
- Lightweight aluminum head assembly (Aluminum 5005 H34 turbine/throat)
- Sealed double-row ball bearing (main rotation)
- Spider bearing on H600–H900 for added stability
- Wind speed rating 205.2 km/h (57 m/s), Performance Level 1 per AS 4740:2000
- Optional roof-operated manual damper (open in spring, close in fall)
- 5-year automatic warranty; 15-year extended warranty on registration within 30 days

## What Makes It Different
- **Not a fan** — no motor. The Hurricane is a ventilator, not a circulator. Use it to exhaust air out of a building, not to move air around inside.
- **No control system** — there is nothing to wire or program. The optional damper is manually operated.
- **Passive vs. Ecopower hybrid.** Ecopower uses the same Hurricane-style rotating head but adds a hidden EC motor for guaranteed exhaust rates when there isn't enough wind. See `products/ecopower.md`.

## Power & Electrical Requirements
None. The Hurricane is passive.

## Mounting & Installation Notes

**Sloped roof:**
1. Pick a location. The head must sit above the finished roof ridge for correct operation and to keep the flashing under the ridge cap.
2. Cut the roof opening to match the flue/flashing sizes. Make sure at least one raised/standing seam passes under the flashing for a weather seal.
3. Standard flashing installs up under the ridge flashing. Sealant may be used between metal roof, ridge cap, and flashings.
4. On sloped roofs a Vari-Pitch Flue Section must be installed. Rotate the Vari-Pitch until the throat is horizontal (use a level). Fasten the two halves with 4 tek screws.
5. (Optional) install a Straight Flue on top of the Vari-Pitch.
6. Install the Turbine Head.

**Flat roof:**
- Turbine head → straight flue section → standard base flashing → roof membrane
- Curb flashing (optional, built by others) around the opening
- Roofing material must run up the face of the curb flashing and be weather sealed at the edge joints

**Recommended fastener quantities (10-gauge 16 mm tek screws with neo, or 1/8" blind rivets):**

| Unit size (mm) | Head → Varipitch | Varipitch → Flashing | Flashing → Roof (4 near Varipitch) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 300 | 6 | 6 | 12 |
| 600 | 9 | 9 | 16 |
| 700 | 9 | 9 | 16 |
| 900 | 12 | 12 | 26 |

If non-sealed rivets are used, apply silicone over the rivets to seal.

## Maintenance Requirements
1. **Annual cleaning** of Hurricane heads and components. Remove foreign matter and debris around the unit to ensure smooth operation.
2. **Inspect** for:
   - Damage and wear
   - Smooth rotation without resistance or non-wind-related noise
   - All components operational
3. **Test head connection** — grasp the Hurricane head on opposing sides and gently try to lift it vertically to confirm minimal movement between the head and the bottom plate.

## Compatible Accessories & Controls

## Common FAQs

**Q: Does the Hurricane need electricity?**
A: No. It is a passive, wind-driven roof ventilator. Nothing to wire.

**Q: What's the biggest Hurricane?**
A: H900 — 36" throat, 43" OD, 63 lbs, 7,331 CFM at 16 km/h wind.

**Q: What wind speed does it need to operate?**
A: It spins whenever there is wind. Exhaust capacity scales with wind speed: the performance table gives values at 6, 12, and 16 km/h. There is no documented minimum wind speed in this wiki.

**Q: Can I install one on a steep-pitched roof?**
A: Yes. Use a Vari-Pitch Flue Section. The H300, H600, and H700 cover 0°–45° roof pitches; the H900 covers 0°–22.5°.

**Q: Can I close the Hurricane for the winter?**
A: Yes — order the straight flue section with a manual roof-operated damper (e.g., EN900X0108 for the H900). The damper lets you open in spring and close in fall.

**Q: What's the wind speed rating?**
A: 205.2 km/h (57 m/s), Performance Level 1 per AS 4740:2000.

**Q: Is there a version with a motor for calm days?**
A: Yes — the Ecopower product is a hybrid ventilator built on the Hurricane's design with a hidden EC motor for guaranteed exhaust rates. See `products/ecopower.md`.

**Q: What's the warranty?**
A: 5-year automatic. 15-year extended warranty available on registration within 30 days of purchase.

**Q: Does the Hurricane work with a BAS?**
A: The passive Hurricane does not. For BAS compatibility with a similar exhaust product, use Ecopower (the 2025 catalogue notes Ecopower is compatible with most BMS systems).

**Q: What fasteners should I use?**
A: 10-gauge 16 mm tek screws with neo washers, or 1/8" blind rivets (seal with silicone if not pre-sealed).
