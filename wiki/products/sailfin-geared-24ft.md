# Altra-Air Sailfin Geared — 24 FT (EN675X5016)

## Overview
The Sailfin Geared 24 FT is the largest geared Altra-Air Sailfin. It shares the 5-blade Sailfin airfoil and Nord helical in-line gearmotor architecture with the rest of the range. Suits very large single-bay facilities that prefer a single high-CFM fan over multiple smaller ones.

## Best Applications
- Very large industrial bays
- Large dairy operations (e.g., Canadian case study: eight 24 FT Sailfins for 200 cows)
- Large manufacturing facilities for heat de-stratification
- Facilities that need the broader geared voltage options (400 V / 575 V)

## Key Features & Benefits
- Highest CFM Sailfin (315,026 CFM) shared with the 24 FT gearless
- Nord helical gearmotor (2.5× stronger blade design per AMCA testing)
- 2 HP, 200°C ISR VFD-duty winding
- 5-blade Sailfin airfoil with up to 23° angle of attack; blade top width 10¼" for maximum airflow
- 4 voltage options
- Creates an evaporative cooling effect of 3 to 4°C throughout the facility
- UL, CSA, and CE certified
- No drive belts or belt tighteners to maintain
- Maximum 5.7 A draw; 264 CFM/Watt efficiency; minimal 2.5" blade flex (per AMCA testing)

## What Makes It Different (vs. 24 FT Gearless)
- Drive: gearmotor vs. direct drive
- Maintenance: gear-oil check at 18 months / change every 20,000 hrs vs. none
- Noise: louder vs. whisper quiet
- LED: not included vs. 20,000 lumen standard
- Voltage: 4 options vs. 2
- Same CFM (315,026), same RPM (53), same HP (2), same weight (157 kg)

## Power & Electrical Requirements
- One VFD per fan. 230/400/460/575 V.
- VFD handles overtemp/overload; do not use motor thermal wires.
- Separate insulated ground to each VFD.
- Motor insulation class F.
- Lenze cable-length rules: see `guides/electrical-install-lenze.md`.

## Mounting & Installation Notes
- 10 ft minimum clearance.
- Mount types: OWSJ, Purlin Z, I-Beam, Wood Beam, Concrete Beam.
- Drops: 12"/24"/48" standard; custom available.
- 4-point SS guy wires at 45°–60°, ~90° apart; no turnbuckles.
- Safety cables at every connection.
- 1 ft min VFD-to-fan separation.
- **Pre-start:** remove the rubber vent plug from the gearbox.

## Maintenance Requirements
- Gear oil: check at 18 months; change every 20,000 hrs.
- Motor/blade/mount/VFD schedule per `guides/maintenance-schedule.md`.
- Assumes ~5,000 hrs/year; tighten for aggressive environments.

## Compatible Accessories & Controls
- EssentialAIR, ZoneAIR, CommandAIR, ModAIR, Schneider VFD, SmartAIR, TouchAIR, LVC + TFD-1, Wind Sensor (mandatory agricultural), Fire relay (optional).

## Common FAQs

**Q: Do the geared and gearless 24 FT move the same amount of air?**
A: Yes. Both are 315,026 CFM at 53 RPM, per the 2022 spec sheets. The differences are drive, maintenance, noise, LED, and voltage options.

**Q: What's the weight for mounting calculations?**
A: 157 kg (347 lbs). Same for geared and gearless.

**Q: How often do I change the oil?**
A: Oil level check at 18 months; full oil change every 20,000 hours of operation.

**Q: Does it include an LED light?**
A: No — LED is only on gearless Sailfins.

**Q: What voltages does it support?**
A: 230, 400, 460, or 575 V.

**Q: Can I dim the LED with a SmartAIR?**
A: This unit has no LED. If you want a Sailfin with an LED that SmartAIR can dim, look at the 24 FT gearless (EN670X5016).

**Q: What's the pre-start step I have to remember?**
A: Remove the rubber vent plug from the gearbox and discard it. Missing this is a known issue on geared Sailfins.

## Blade Aerodynamic Advantage
- Stall angles as high as 23°
- Always stalls gradually — eliminating tip stalling (the primary cause of blade noise and damaging vibration)
- Eliminates span-wise pumping — the primary cause of efficiency loss in all rotating systems
- Hyper-stability lowers noise and vibrations that cause wear on blades and drive train
- No other blade can match the Sailfin design for generating substantially greater lift while reducing drag

## Clearance Requirements (Dairy Catalogue)
- Min 60" (152 cm) centre of fan to roof deck for ideal operating performance
- Min 30" (76 cm) from fan blade's leading edge to any obstruction above or below fan
- Min 18" (46 cm) from side of fan to any obstruction
- Min 144" (366 cm) floor to fan leading edge height
- Ideal height of 180" (457 cm) from floor to blades (eliminates safety issues with equipment)

## Agricultural / Hog Barn Applications
- Provides constant mixing of air within the room
- Inlets counter-balanced and located directly above the fan
- Fans distribute fresh air — leading to a 30% reduction in exhaust capacity required
- More air speed reduces fly issues
- Reverse capability enables winter circulation without drafts

## Natural Ventilation Integration
When curtains are open and fans are utilized, fans push incoming air down and move it in all directions. Air exits out the side walls at a lower level, allowing fresh air to enter at the top of the opening following the ceiling line. Fans move air in a circular pattern — natural wind direction does not matter. During cold winter periods, fans can be operated at very low speed to slowly mix air without drafts, reducing freezing on outside alley floors.
