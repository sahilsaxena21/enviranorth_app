# Altra-Air Sailfin Geared — 16 FT (EN675X5010)

## Overview
The Sailfin Geared 16 FT is a mid-size HVLS ceiling fan with a Nord helical in-line gearmotor. It shares the 5-blade Sailfin airfoil with the rest of the range. The 16 FT size is one of the more common mid-range choices for medium industrial bays and medium-size dairy barns.

## Best Applications
- Medium industrial bays and manufacturing cells
- Medium dairy barns and livestock spaces
- Warehouses where the 12 FT is too small but 20 FT is wider than the structural bay
- Facilities on 400 V or 575 V supplies (geared lineup only)

## Key Features & Benefits
- Nord helical in-line gearmotor (2.5× stronger blade design per AMCA testing)
- 5-blade Sailfin airfoil with up to 23° angle of attack; blade top width 10¼" for maximum airflow
- 1.5 HP — a step up from the 1 HP 12 FT
- 200°C ISR VFD-duty motor winding
- 4 voltage options (230/400/460/575 V)
- Lifetime warranty on blades and hub
- Creates an evaporative cooling effect of 3 to 4°C throughout the facility
- UL, CSA, and CE certified
- No drive belts or belt tighteners to maintain

## What Makes It Different (vs. 16 FT Gearless)
- 1.5 HP (geared) vs. 2 HP (gearless)
- Same 125 kg weight in the source spec sheet
- No LED light (gearless includes 20,000 lumen LED)
- Oil maintenance required (check 18 months, change every 20,000 hrs)
- Louder than gearless; 4 voltage options vs. 2

## Power & Electrical Requirements
- One VFD per fan. 230/400/460/575 V options.
- VFD provides over-temperature and overload protection; motor thermal wires not used.
- Separate insulated ground to each VFD.
- Motor insulation class F.
- Lenze VFD cable-length rules: 250 ft @ 230 V, 160 ft @ 460 V, 125 ft @ 600 V without extras. See `guides/electrical-install-lenze.md`.

## Mounting & Installation Notes
- 10 ft minimum clearance.
- Mount types: OWSJ, Purlin Z, I-Beam, Wood Beam, Concrete Beam.
- Drops: 12"/24"/48" standard; custom available.
- 4-point SS guy wires at 45°–60°, ~90° apart, no turnbuckles.
- Safety cables at every connection.
- 1 ft minimum VFD-to-fan separation.
- **Pre-start:** remove the rubber vent plug from the gearbox before first start-up.

## Maintenance Requirements
- Gear oil: check at 18 months; change every 20,000 hrs.
- Motor/blade/mount/VFD schedule same as all Sailfins — see `guides/maintenance-schedule.md`.

## Compatible Accessories & Controls
- EssentialAIR, ZoneAIR, CommandAIR, ModAIR, Schneider VFD, SmartAIR, TouchAIR, LVC + TFD-1, Wind Sensor (mandatory agricultural), Fire relay (optional).

## Common FAQs

**Q: What is the HP rating of the 16 FT geared?**
A: 1.5 HP. The 16 FT gearless is 2 HP.

**Q: Does it include an LED light?**
A: No — LED is only included on gearless Sailfins.

**Q: Do I have to change gear oil?**
A: Yes. Check at 18 months, change every 20,000 hours of normal operation.

**Q: What voltages are supported?**
A: 230 V, 400 V, 460 V, and 575 V. Single-phase is supported at 230 V via the Schneider VFD.

**Q: What's the minimum ceiling height?**
A: 10 ft (3.05 m) from blade leading edge to floor. Unguarded impeller — not for accessible spaces.

**Q: Can I run this fan with a SmartAIR touchscreen?**
A: Yes, via a Schneider VFD or via ModAIR (Lenze + ModBus). EssentialAIR/ZoneAIR/CommandAIR are Lenze-only, analogue control paths.

**Q: How loud is it compared to the gearless?**
A: The geared Sailfins are louder (62–63 dB class) than the gearless (45 dB class, described as whisper quiet). If acoustics matter, pick gearless.

## Blade Aerodynamic Advantage
- Stall angles as high as 23°
- Always stalls gradually — eliminating tip stalling (the primary cause of blade noise and damaging vibration)
- Eliminates span-wise pumping — the primary cause of efficiency loss in all rotating systems
- Hyper-stability lowers noise and vibrations that cause wear on blades and drive train

## Clearance Requirements (Dairy Catalogue)
- Min 60" (152 cm) centre of fan to roof deck for ideal operating performance
- Min 30" (76 cm) from fan blade's leading edge to any obstruction above or below fan
- Min 18" (46 cm) from side of fan to any obstruction
- Min 144" (366 cm) floor to fan leading edge height
- Ideal height of 180" (457 cm) from floor to blades (eliminates safety issues with equipment)

## Dairy Applications
- Higher average wind speeds than traditional cooling fans
- Reduces cow crowding; increases milk production; reduces heat stress
- Minimizes the impact of heat on cow fertility
- Automatic controls allow the fan to only operate when necessary — reducing energy costs
- Fans are ceiling-mounted out of the way of moving equipment
- Moving air reduces birds and flies in the facility
- Less wiring and labour required vs. traditional cooling fans
- 4-Row and 6-Row dairy barn layouts supported (see Dairy Catalogue for fan placement diagrams)

- Provides constant mixing of air within the room
- Inlets counter-balanced and located directly above the fan
- Fans distribute fresh air — leading to a 30% reduction in exhaust capacity required
- More air speed reduces fly issues
- Reverse capability enables winter circulation without drafts
