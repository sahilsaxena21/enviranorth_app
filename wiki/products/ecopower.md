# Ecopower Hybrid Roof Ventilator (EN921X8220)

## Overview
Ecopower is a hybrid roof ventilator that combines Envira-North's Hurricane passive-turbine design with a hidden EC (electronically commutated) motor. When there's enough wind, it behaves like a Hurricane — free-spinning and passive. When there isn't, the EC motor drives the turbine to guarantee a minimum exhaust rate. It's the "best of both worlds" option when you need reliable ventilation but also want to take advantage of free wind energy when it's available.

## Best Applications
- Apartment buildings and multi-unit residential needing reliable ventilation shafts (case study: 73 Ecopower units installed in a Montreal apartment building, hybrid ventilation shafts, minimal noise, helped qualify for Green Building Certification)
- Commercial and institutional buildings with a BMS in place that want automated control over ventilation
- Facilities where passive Hurricane output is not consistent enough but running a fully powered exhaust fan all the time would waste energy

## Key Features & Benefits
- Uses the same rotating-head design as the Hurricane — free-spinning under wind load
- Hidden EC motor — not visible from the roofline, preserving the Hurricane's profile
- Extremely efficient (per the 2025 catalogue: "extremely efficient," "outperforms competitors")
- Lightweight and quiet
- Compatible with most BMS systems
- Minimal maintenance
- Dependable ventilation regardless of wind conditions

## What Makes It Different
- **vs. Hurricane:** Hurricane is purely passive (no power, no control). Ecopower adds a hidden EC motor so you can guarantee an exhaust rate when the wind isn't cooperating. Ecopower has wiring and can talk to a BMS; Hurricane has neither.
- **vs. a powered roof exhaust fan:** Ecopower can still spin free under wind load when the motor isn't running, saving power. A standard exhaust fan draws power whenever it runs.
- **Aesthetic** — the motor is hidden, so the roofline looks like a Hurricane, not like a powered exhaust fan.
