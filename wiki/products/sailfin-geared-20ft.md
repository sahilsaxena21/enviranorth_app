# Altra-Air Sailfin Geared — 20 FT (EN675X5014)

## Overview
The Sailfin Geared 20 FT is one of the large HVLS ceiling fans in the Sailfin range, driven by a Nord helical in-line gearmotor. Example deployments in the Design & Engineering brochure include a 100,000 sq ft Toronto facility that used five 20 FT Sailfin fans for de-stratification and cut heating costs ~30 %.

## Best Applications
- Large manufacturing and warehouse bays
- Large dairy barns
- High-ceiling arenas and recreational facilities
- Heating / de-stratification applications (per the Toronto case study)

## Key Features & Benefits
- Nord helical gearmotor — robust, widely used in industrial ventilation (2.5× stronger blade design per AMCA testing)
- 5-blade Sailfin airfoil with up to 23° angle of attack; blade top width 10¼" for maximum airflow
- 2 HP (same as 20 FT gearless)
- 4 voltage options (230/400/460/575 V)
- Lifetime blade and hub warranty
- Creates an evaporative cooling effect of 3 to 4°C throughout the facility
- UL, CSA, and CE certified
- No drive belts or belt tighteners to maintain

## What Makes It Different (vs. 20 FT Gearless)
- Drive: gearmotor vs. direct drive
- No LED light (gearless includes 20,000 lumen LED)
- Oil maintenance (check 18 months, change every 20,000 hrs) vs. none on gearless
- Louder than gearless
- 4 voltage options vs. 2 on gearless
- Both 20 FT variants: 2 HP, 176,200 CFM, 63 RPM, 140 kg

## Power & Electrical Requirements
- One VFD per fan. 230/400/460/575 V.
- VFD handles overtemp/overload — do not use motor thermal wires.
- Separate insulated ground per VFD.
- Motor insulation class F.
- Lenze cable-length rules: see `guides/electrical-install-lenze.md`.

## Mounting & Installation Notes
- 10 ft minimum clearance.
- Mount types: OWSJ, Purlin Z, I-Beam, Wood Beam, Concrete Beam.
- Drops: 12"/24"/48" standard; custom available.
- 4-point SS guy wires at 45°–60°, ~90° apart, no turnbuckles.
- Safety cables at every connection.
- 1 ft minimum VFD-to-fan separation.
- **Pre-start:** remove the rubber vent plug from the gearbox.

## Maintenance Requirements
- Gear oil: check at 18 months; change every 20,000 hrs of normal operation.
- Motor/blade/mount/VFD schedule per `guides/maintenance-schedule.md`.

## Compatible Accessories & Controls
- EssentialAIR, ZoneAIR, CommandAIR, ModAIR, Schneider VFD, SmartAIR, TouchAIR, LVC, TFD-1, Wind Sensor (mandatory agricultural), Fire relay (optional).

## Common FAQs

**Q: What CFM does the 20 FT geared produce?**
A: 176,200 CFM at 63 RPM full speed — same as the 20 FT gearless.

**Q: What's the HP?**
A: 2 HP. Same as the 20 FT gearless.

**Q: How many 20 FT geared fans were used in the Toronto de-stratification case study?**
A: Five 20 FT Sailfin fans in a 100,000 sq ft facility, for a reported ~30 % heating-cost reduction. The brochure does not specify geared vs. gearless for this case — contact Envira-North for project specifics.

**Q: Do I need a separate controller for each of these fans?**
A: Each fan needs its own VFD. For multi-fan control from one point: LVC (up to 7 fans on ZoneAIR, or up to 10 Schneider VFDs), SmartAIR Multi-Fan (up to 10 fans), or TouchAIR (up to 20 fans).

**Q: Does it include an LED light?**
A: No. LED is included on gearless Sailfins only.

**Q: What's the weight for mounting calculations?**
A: 140 kg (320 lbs).

## Blade Aerodynamic Advantage
- Stall angles as high as 23°
- Always stalls gradually — eliminating tip stalling (the primary cause of blade noise and damaging vibration)
- Eliminates span-wise pumping — the primary cause of efficiency loss in all rotating systems
- Hyper-stability lowers noise and vibrations that cause wear on blades and drive train

## Clearance Requirements (Dairy Catalogue)
- Min 60" (152 cm) centre of fan to roof deck for ideal operating performance
- Min 30" (76 cm) from fan blade's leading edge to any obstruction above or below fan
- Min 18" (46 cm) from side of fan to any obstruction
- Min 144" (366 cm) floor to fan leading edge height
- Ideal height of 180" (457 cm) from floor to blades (eliminates safety issues with equipment)

## Dairy Applications
- Recommended for 4-Row and 6-Row dairy barn layouts (see Dairy Catalogue for fan placement diagrams)
- Each row of 20 ft fans centred between the sidewall and centre of barn to maximize air movement
- Reduces cow crowding; increases milk production; reduces heat stress
- Minimizes the impact of heat on cow fertility
- Fans are ceiling-mounted out of the way of moving equipment
- Moving air reduces birds and flies in the facility
- Can be combined with high-pressure misting in areas of low humidity

## Agricultural / Hog Barn Applications
- Provides constant mixing of air within the room
- Inlets counter-balanced and located directly above the fan
- Fans distribute fresh air — leading to a 30% reduction in exhaust capacity required
- More air speed reduces fly issues
- Reverse capability enables winter circulation without drafts

## Natural Ventilation Integration
When curtains are open and fans are utilized, the fans push incoming air down and move it in all directions. Air exits out the side walls at a lower level, allowing fresh air to enter at the top of the opening following the ceiling line. Fans move air in a circular pattern — natural wind direction does not matter. During cold winter periods, fans can be operated at very low speed to slowly mix air without drafts.
