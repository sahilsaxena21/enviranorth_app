# General Mounting Rules for Envira-North HVLS Fans

## Overview
This guide collects the cross-product mounting rules that apply to all Envira-North HVLS fans (Sailfin, Jazz, Alite). Product-specific mounting details are in each product page.

## Minimum clearances (Sailfin)
- **Blade-to-floor:** 10 ft (3.05 m) minimum — applies to all Sailfin sizes (8–24 ft). The lowest moving part must be at least 3.05 m (10 ft) above floor or grade level.
- **Blade-to-ceiling / deck:** follow the mounting kit spec for the specific structure type (OWSJ, Purlin Z, wood, beam)
- **Blade-to-wall / obstruction:** half the fan diameter, minimum
- **Fan-to-fan spacing:** 2.5× the fan diameter centre-to-centre for even airflow coverage

> **CAUTION (from installation guide):** This unit has an unguarded impeller. Do not use in locations readily accessible to people or animals. Install fan so that the blade is at least 3.05 meters (10 feet) above the floor. Support directly from building structure.

## Jazz clearances
- Jazz fans are IP54 / NEMA 3, indoor only
- Follow the Jazz installation guide for specific ceiling-to-blade clearance by size

## Structural requirements
- **Verify structural capacity** — every HVLS installation requires a structural review of the mounting point. The dynamic load includes fan weight plus the imbalance / thrust load.
- **Safety cable (Sailfin):** a secondary restraint cable is required on every Sailfin. This is a safety device, not an electrical bond.
- **Mounting kit:** use the Envira-North mounting kit matched to the structure type. Do not fabricate a kit on site.

> **Warning (from installation guide):** Use of any other mounting hardware or technique is strictly prohibited. Only use mounting hardware/brackets supplied by Envira-North.

## Mounting types (Sailfin)
Five mounting configurations are available for the Altra-Air Sailfin:

| Mount Type | Hardware |
|---|---|
| **Standard I-Beam / OWSJ** | Universal I-Beam Clamp w/ Swivel; mfg "I" Beam clamps and spacers |
| **Purlin "Z" Mount** | Steel Beam Brackets |
| **Wood Beam Mount** | Steel Machined Mount Plates |
| **Concrete Beam Mount** | Steel Beam with Matching Mount Plates |
| **Open Web Steel Joist (OWSJ)** | Steel Beam With Brackets |

Steel thickness varies depending on beam span — consult factory for specifics.

## Standard Mount drop lengths
Standard drop mounts are available in:
- **12" (1 ft)** — standard
- **24" (2 ft)** — standard
- **48" (4 ft)** — standard
- **Custom lengths** — optional, contact factory

Drop extensions (2 ft and 4 ft) are available to increase overall drop length. If a specific drop distance is required, a custom extension can be ordered.

## Standard Mount hardware package
The Standard Mount package is used with all Altra-Air assemblies (except Wood Beam mounting). It includes:
- (1) Upper Pivot Plate (pre-assembled)
- (1) Upper Pivot (pre-assembled)
- (1) Stem for 12", 24" & 48" packages
- (2) mfg "I" Beam clamps
- (2) mfg "I" Beam spacers (may not be required, depending on beam thickness)
- (4) Bolts ½" x 2½" UNC
- (8) Washers Flat ½"
- (4) Nylock ½" UNC
- (1) Cable 3/16" Stainless Steel, Length 6.5 ft (safety cable)
- (4) Cable Clamps 3/16"

## Extension hardware package
Each extension package includes:
- (1) Extension (2 ft or 4 ft)
- (4) Bolts ½" x 1½" UNC
- (8) Washers Flat ½"
- (4) Nylocks ½" UNC
- (1) Cable 3/16" Stainless Steel, Length 3 ft (safety cable)
- (4) Cable Clamps 3/16"

Every connection between components (mounts, extensions, and fan frame) must include a safety cable.

## Guy wire hardware and installation
Guy wires prevent unnecessary sway of the fan in case of unbalanced blades and large movements due to wind or draft.

**Included in the package:**
- (4) Cable 1/8" Stainless Steel (60 ft total, to make 4 × 15 ft runs)
- (8) Thimbles 1/4" Stainless Steel
- (16) Cable Clamps 1/8"

**Extra hardware required (not included — must be sourced separately):**
- (4) Forged Eye Bolts 1/4"
- (4) Nuts 1/4"
- (4) Nylocks 1/4"
- (8) Washers Flat 1/4"

**Guy wire installation rules:**
- Cable angle: **45° to 60°** from the fan frame to the ceiling/beam attachment point
- Guy wires must be fastened to a **structural component** of the building at the specified angle
- Guy cables should be **taut but not over-stressed** — allow free self-leveling
- Wires should be approximately **90° apart** from each other (4 wires at 90° spacing)
- **DO NOT USE TURNBUCKLES** on guy wires
- For fans hanging more than 10 ft below where the guy wires will mount, additional cable is required
- Fans positioned 4 ft down from ceiling: mount cables approximately 4 ft away from fan horizontally (to maintain 45°–60° angle)

**Cable clamp installation rule:** The U-bolt side of the clip must be placed on the **short turn-back side** and the saddle goes on the **long side (the "live" end)**.

## Tools required to install (from installation guide)
- Level
- Cable cutters (for stainless steel aircraft cable)
- Ratchet or impact gun
- Basic imperial socket set (up to 3/4")
- Basic imperial wrench set
- Lifting devices or scaffolding

## Required steps before installation
1. Check to see that all tools required for installation are present
2. Verify that all fan components were received
3. Check drawings and layouts to locate where the fan is to be installed
4. Ensure work area is safe and all security, policies, and procedures for the facility are met
5. Inspect the lift device or mobile platform
6. Each person installing must use a **safety harness at all times**
7. All workspace safety requirements, lock-out procedures, and hoarding of construction zone must be met and followed

## Agricultural-specific rules
- **Wind sensor is mandatory** on all agricultural installations (see `controls/wind-sensor.md`)
- In open-wall barns, verify the fan is positioned so high winds don't hit the blades broadside at full RPM
- Corrosion: specify the appropriate blade and hardware finish for caustic environments (hog, poultry, dairy) — contact Customer Support

## Cable separation at mounting
- Run motor power and communication cables in separate conduits wherever possible
- On a fan-mounted VFD (Schneider / Lenze ZoneAIR / CommandAIR / ModAIR), the motor cable is very short — the separation concern is the CAT-5 / LVC run from the HMI to the VFD
- Never share a conduit between the 0–10 V LVC signal and motor power

## Lock-out / tag-out before mounting work
- De-energise the drive and lock the disconnect
- Wait 5 minutes after disconnect for Jazz drive capacitors to discharge
- Verify zero voltage at the drive input before touching the motor or motor cable

## Commissioning after mounting
- **Geared Sailfin:** remove the rubber gearbox vent plug before first start-up. Failing to do so builds pressure in the gearbox and can blow a seal.
- **All fans:** run at low speed first, observe for wobble / imbalance, then ramp to full speed
- **Record** the commissioning date for the maintenance schedule (6 months initial inspection, then every 18 months — see `guides/maintenance-schedule.md`)

## Common pitfalls
- Mounting to a purlin without a structural review — the purlin may not be rated for the dynamic load
- Forgetting the gearbox vent plug on a geared Sailfin
- Running the CAT-5 / LVC in the same conduit as motor power
- Installing indoors-only fans (Jazz, Alite) in covered outdoor areas with high humidity
